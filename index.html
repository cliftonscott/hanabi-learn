Basics

  • Work together.
  • You cannot see your own hand at the table.
  • Clues must name one color or one rank.
  • Wrong plays spend fuses.

Rules Reference

Standard cooperative Hanabi, with v1 practice using 3 players and visible cards for learning.

Goal

Build one firework stack per color from 1 to 5. The team score is the sum of the highest card in each stack, up to 25.

Setup

Use 5 colors. Each color has three 1s, two 2s, two 3s, two 4s, and one 5. Use 8 clue tokens and 3 fuse tokens.

Hands

Players hold cards facing away from themselves. With 2-3 players each hand has 5 cards. With 4-5 players each hand has 4 cards.

Turn Actions

On your turn, choose exactly one action: give a clue, discard one card, or play one card.

Clues

A clue must target another player, spend 1 clue token, name one color or one rank, and identify every matching card in that hand.

Discards

You may discard a card to regain 1 clue token, up to 8. If all 8 clue tokens are already available, discard is not a legal action.

Playing Cards

A card succeeds only if it is the next needed rank in its color stack. A failed play goes to the discard pile and costs 1 fuse.

Game End

The game ends when the team completes all fireworks, loses the last fuse, or the deck runs out and each player takes one final turn.
